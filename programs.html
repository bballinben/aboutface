Project G.R.O.W.

GROW stands for Gardening, Rehabilitation, Outreach, and Wellness. AboutFace offers more than group sessions and family empowerment. We now offer vocational training. Project GROW is for veterans and other adults with special needs (ages 14 and up) who want to be self-sufficient as entrepreneurs or employees. Teams of two or three people work together, with the veteran as the team leader. They learn about hydroponic gardening and nutrition, horse farming and fodder system management, and more (equine therapy, bee-keeping, chicken/egg production, and farm equipment maintenance). This vocational course includes business management, business finance, and marketing, and entrepreneurial skills training and coaching.

Project GROW is a partnership effort between AboutFace and Bearfoot Ranch. Our expectation is to collaborate with other community horse farms to incorporate project GROW and encourage the hiring of veterans and other adults with special needs.

Recource Directory

We have many additional recources and work with other organizations to help and guide you. As AboutFace grows, health and wealth education, support programs and other activities will be added to our list of services. If you were hoping to find a program that isn’t listed above, please contact us.

  • Simultaneous treatment for substance use disorders and PTSD has been shown to alleviate symptoms of both disorders and often is the only lifeline to save people drowning in the afflictions of the disorders. https://www.drugrehab.com/co-occurring-disorder/ptsd/
  • Connecting Wounded Warriors, Service Members, Veterans, Their Families and Caregivers with Those Who Support Them. Register as a community provider and/or browse local resources here: www.ebenefits.va.gov/ebenefits/nrd
  • Connects Veterans in crisis and their families and friends with qualified, caring VA responders. Veterans and their loved ones can call 1-800-273-8255 and Press 1. www.veteranscrisisline.net
  • The VA Enrollment Website is the fastest and easiest way to apply for VA care or update information. 1-877-222-VETS (8387) www.1010ez.med.va.gov
  • My HealtheVet is the VA’s Personal Health Record. It was designed for Veterans, Servicemembers, their dependents and caregivers. When you register on My HealtheVet, you gain access to tools that can help you track and monitor your health. www.myhealth.va.gov
  • Provides support for caregivers of Veterans and information about services that may be available to them. 1-855-260-3274 www.caregiver.va.gov
  • A VA service to help family members and friends help a Veteran seek VA care. Callers get information on VA services and coaching on talking about treatment needs with the Veteran in their lives. 1-888-823-7458 www.va.gov/coachingintocare
  • Each VA medical center has a homeless coordinator and programs that help Veterans establish or maintain safe, stable housing. Call VA’s National Call Center for Homeless Veterans (1-877-424-3838), a toll-free hotline available 24 hours, 7 days a week. www.va.gov/homeless
  • Women Veterans hotline—1-855-VA-WOMEN (1-855-829-6636)—an incoming call center that receives and responds to questions from women Veterans, their families, and caregivers across the nation about available VA services and resources. www.womenshealth.va.gov
  • Readjustment counseling services are available at 300 community-based offices (Vet Centers) across the nation. 1-877-WAR-VETS (927-8387) www.vetcenter.va.gov
